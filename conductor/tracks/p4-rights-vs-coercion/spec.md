# Specification: P4 - Rights vs Coercion

## Title
Rights versus Coercion in Mental Health Law

## Research Question
Has rights language displaced coercive language in mental health legislation?

## Abstract (Draft)
Mental health legislation balances individual rights with protective interventions. This study analyzes the evolution of rights-based versus coercive language in NZ mental health legislation from 2000-2026 using corpus linguistics methods. We track language shifts to inform policy debate ahead of the 2026 election and ongoing mental health law reform.

---

## Corpus Scope

### Inclusion Criteria
- **Primary legislation:** Mental Health Act and amendments
- **Related legislation:** Health and Disability Commissioner, Protection of Personal and Property Rights
- **Versions:** All versions 2000-2026

### Key Provisions
- Compulsory treatment provisions
- Rights statements
- Safeguard mechanisms
- Review processes

## Analysis Methods

### Primary Metrics
1. **Rights Language:** Rights, autonomy, consent, choice frequency
2. **Coercive Language:** Compulsory, detain, restrain, mandate frequency
3. **Balance Ratio:** Rights/coercion ratio over time
4. **Safeguard Provisions:** Review, appeal, advocacy mechanisms

### Coding Schema
- **Language type:** Rights-based, coercive, neutral, procedural
- **Provision type:** Admission, treatment, review, discharge
- **Stakeholder:** Patient, clinician, family, tribunal
