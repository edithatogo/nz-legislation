# Specification: P2 - Accountability Drift

## Title
Responsibility and Accountability Drift

## Research Question
How have responsibilities shifted across institutions after Pae Ora?

## Abstract (Draft)
The Pae Ora (Healthy Futures) Act 2022 restructured New Zealand's health system, dissolving district health boards and creating new entities. This study analyzes shifts in institutional responsibility and accountability mechanisms in NZ health legislation before and after Pae Ora using corpus linguistics methods. We track responsibility drift across governance structures to inform policy debate ahead of the 2026 election.

---

## Corpus Scope

### Inclusion Criteria
- **Primary legislation:** Health-related Acts 2000-2026
- **Focus:** Pae Ora Act 2022 and related amendments
- **Secondary legislation:** Regulations on governance, accountability
- **Versions:** Pre- and post-Pae Ora versions

### Exclusion Criteria
- Non-health legislation
- Clinical guidelines (non-legislative)
- Repealed provisions outside scope

## Analysis Methods

### Primary Metrics
1. **Responsibility Attribution:** Entity mentions per provision
2. **Accountability Mechanisms:** Reporting, oversight, complaint provisions
3. **Institutional Change:** Before/after Pae Ora comparison
4. **Drift Index:** Composite measure of responsibility shift

### Coding Schema
- **Entity type:** Ministry, DHB, Te Whatu Ora, Iwi, other
- **Responsibility type:** Service delivery, funding, oversight, advocacy
- **Accountability type:** Reporting, audit, complaint, review
