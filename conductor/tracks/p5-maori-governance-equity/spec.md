# Specification: P5 - Māori Governance Equity

## Title
Māori Governance and Equity Language

## Research Question
How has Māori governance and equity language evolved in New Zealand health law?

## Abstract (Draft)
Te Tiriti o Waitangi implementation in health legislation has evolved significantly since 2000. This study analyzes the evolution of Māori governance, partnership, and equity language in NZ health legislation from 2000-2026 using corpus linguistics methods. We track language shifts to inform policy debate ahead of the 2026 election and ongoing health system reform.

---

## Corpus Scope

### Inclusion Criteria
- **Primary legislation:** Health-related Acts 2000-2026
- **Key legislation:** Pae Ora, NZ Public Health and Disability Act
- **Versions:** All versions in scope period

### Key Provisions
- Te Tiriti references
- Māori participation provisions
- Equity statements
- Partnership mechanisms

## Analysis Methods

### Primary Metrics
1. **Te Tiriti Language:** Treaty, partnership, protection frequency
2. **Māori Governance:** Iwi, hapū, whānau, Māori Authority mentions
3. **Equity Language:** Equity, disparity, outcomes mentions
4. **Implementation Mechanisms:** Specific provisions for Māori participation

### Coding Schema
- **Language type:** Te Tiriti, governance, equity, general
- **Provision type:** Aspirational, mandatory, procedural
- **Mechanism type:** Advisory, decision-making, funding, oversight
